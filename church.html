Catholic

Pope

The Pope is the only primate for all Catholics. The Pope is also a secular ruler of a realm known as the Papal States. Rome is normally the capital of the Papal States. The Pope is elected from all Catholic Cardinals. Only Cardinals can be elected Pope and only the Pope installs the title of Cardinal to Bishops, Prince-Bishops, Archbishops, and Prince-Archbishops.

If a Catholic realm that has the status of Kingdom or Empire is sufficiently powerful and has Cardinals, then it can install an Antipope. An Antipope is not recognised by other Catholics.

The Pope can excommunicate Catholics and can install an Anti-King.

Cardinal

The current Pope can elevate a Bishop or Archbishop to the rank of Cardinal. All the Cardinals elect the next Pope when the former is deceased or the seat vacant.

Orthodox

Patriarch

Each orthodox realm that has the title of Tsar or Emperor can have a Patriarch. The Patriarch is elected from one of all Bishops or Archbishops within that particular Orthodox realm. An Orthodox realm that is secular indepedent, but has not reached the secular status of an empire, is a dependency of another patriarchate.

Islam

Caliph

A Caliph is also a secular leader of a muslim realm. Though a Caliph was initially elected from a Shura, the succession is dynastic. For the Turks the highest secular leader is the Sultan. A Sultan can also claim a Caliphate, but keeps the title of Sultan.
